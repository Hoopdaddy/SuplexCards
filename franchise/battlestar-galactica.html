So Say We All

Ronald D. Moore's critically acclaimed 2003 reimagining of Glen A. Larson's original 1978 series is widely considered one of the finest science fiction dramas ever produced. Set in a distant star system, the series follows the remnants of humanity — survivors of a devastating Cylon attack on the Twelve Colonies — fleeing across the galaxy in a ragtag fleet led by the aging battlestar Galactica.

The reimagined series tackled themes of identity, religion, democracy, terrorism, and survival with a moral complexity rarely seen in science fiction television. It earned a prestigious Peabody Award and a place in the United Nations for a special screening of its episodes dealing with human rights.

BSG collectibles include Eaglemoss' die-cast Battlestar and Cylon Raider ship models, Diamond Select statues, and Character Options' detailed figure lines.

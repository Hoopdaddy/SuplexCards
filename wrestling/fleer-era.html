Fleer Years

The bridge era — from WWF to WWE, Fleer documents the Ruthless Aggression generation's birth and the first Cena, Lesnar, and Orton cards

Fleer Sets & Checklists

Where the Modern Hobby Was Born

When Fleer secured the exclusive WWE license in 2001, wrestling cards would never be the same. Out went the simple base-set-and-checklist formula that had defined the hobby for two decades. In came the tools that redefined what a trading card could be — relic cards packed with ring-worn gear, certified on-card autographs from the sport's biggest names, and hit-driven insert sets that made ripping packs an event. The era of the hit card had arrived.

Fleer's four-year run coincided with one of the most seismic moments in sports entertainment history — the WWF becoming WWE, the dawn of Ruthless Aggression, and the emergence of a new generation. Brock Lesnar. John Cena. Randy Orton. Their earliest cards live in these sets. Short-lived but deeply influential, the Fleer era laid the blueprint for every premium wrestling card release that came after it.

My All-Time Top 10 Cards to Own

#1 — 1985 Topps WWF Hulk Hogan #1

The card that started it all. The king of vintage wrestling cards. A PSA 9 copy is the crown jewel of any collection.

VintageGrail

#2 — 1990 Classic WWF Ultimate Warrior RC #3

The Warrior's first major card. Iconic image, underrated value. Climbing fast as nostalgia peaks.

VintageHot

#3 — 1985 Topps WWF Randy Savage RC #2

"Ooh yeah!" The Macho Man's rookie card. Pop from the top rope — this card is always in demand.

Vintage

#4 — 1997 Cardz ECW Hardcore Heaven RVD RC

One of the rarest wrestling RCs in the hobby. Mr. Monday Night before Monday Night. Extremely hard to find.

VintageECWRare

#5 — 1994 Action Packed WWF Bret Hart Auto

The Hitman's certified auto from the embossed era. Short printed, on-card signature. Few survive in great shape.

VintageWWF

#6 — 1995 Cardz WCW Steve Austin RC

Stone Cold before Stone Cold — as "Stunning" Steve Austin. One of the most sought WCW cards from the era.

VintageWCW

#7 — 2015 Topps WWE NXT Sasha Banks RC

The Boss's NXT rookie card. Women's wrestling card market has never been hotter. This one leads the charge.

ModernHot

#8 — 2022 Prizm WWE Cody Rhodes RC Silver

The American Nightmare's WWE return Prizm rookie. Capturing the Undisputed title sent this card through the roof.

ModernHot

#9 — 1999 Topps WCW Goldberg #1

Goldberg. #1 overall. WCW. 173-0. There's no more fitting number for this card. An all-time great piece of cardboard.

VintageWCW

#10 — 2019 Topps NXT Rhea Ripley Auto RC

The Eradicator's NXT rookie auto. Her rise to the top of WWE is one of the great modern stories. Get it cheap now.

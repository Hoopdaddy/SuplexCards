Wrestling History

From the territories to the Thunderdome Ś the greatest story ever told on cardboard

The Eras of Professional Wrestling

Each era shaped the cards we collect. Understanding the history makes the cardboard mean more.

The Golden Era (1984¢1992)

Hulk Hogan. WrestleMania. The birth of WWF as a national phenomenon. This is the era that created wrestling card collecting Ś the 1985 Topps set was the first major wrestling card release and remains the most important set in the hobby.

Vintage WWF

The New Generation (1992¢1997)

Bret Hart. Shawn Michaels. The Undertaker. Diesel. Razor Ramon. WWF evolved after Hogan's departure into a grittier, more athletic product. The Action Packed set from this era produced some of the hobby's most undervalued autographs.

Vintage WWF

The Monday Night Wars (1995¢2001)

WWF vs. WCW. Raw vs. Nitro. Austin vs. McMahon. nWo vs. everyone. The hottest wrestling has ever been. Cards from this era Ś Topps WCW, Cardz WCW, ECW Hardcore Heaven Ś capture lightning in a bottle. Collecting this era is like owning a piece of history.

Vintage WCW ECW

The Attitude Era (1997¢2002)

Stone Cold. The Rock. D-Generation X. Mankind. This era redefined what wrestling could be. The 2000 Fleer WWF WrestleMania set is the era's defining card release Ś packed with Attitude Era rookies who became legends.

Vintage WWF

The Ruthless Aggression Era (2002¢2008)

Brock Lesnar's first run. John Cena's rise. Edge becomes a legend. Randy Orton's evolution. The brand split era brought incredible wrestling and the modern Topps WWE sets begin here. The hobby's modern foundation.

Modern WWE

The PG Era & Reality Era (2008¢2016)

CM Punk's pipebomb. Daniel Bryan's YES Movement. The Shield. The Authority. NXT becomes a thing. The 2012¢2015 Topps WWE sets are some of the best checklist values in modern collecting Ś packed with future legends.

Modern WWE

The Women's Revolution (2015¢present)

Sasha. Bayley. Charlotte. Becky. The Four Horsewomen changed wrestling forever. Women's cards went from afterthoughts to market drivers. The 2015 Topps WWE NXT inserts for Sasha, Becky, Charlotte, and Bayley are the defining RCs of this era.

Modern Hot

The Roman Reigns Era (2020¢present)

The Tribal Chief's heel run is an all-time great character work. Sami Zayn's Honorary Uce story. Cody Rhodes finishing the story. This current era is producing some of the most valuable modern cards in the hobby.

Modern WWE Hot

Wrestling Card History Timeline

1985

Topps WWF Ś The First Major Wrestling Set

66 cards. Wax packs. Hulk Hogan on every pack. The hobby was born. Kids ripped packs hoping for a Hogan, a Savage, or an Andre. Those same cards are now worth hundreds in high grade.

1986¢89

Topps Continues Ś The WWF Monopoly

Topps released WWF sets throughout the late '80s. The 1987 and 1988 sets are underrated and undervalued Ś full of legends at low prices. A great entry point for vintage collectors.

1990¢91

Classic WWF Ś The New Wave

Classic Games enters the scene with 150-card sets. Ultimate Warrior's first card appears here (#3 in the 1990 set). Shawn Michaels, Ted DiBiase, Jake Roberts all get their key cards. Undertaker's first card is in the 1990 set at #57.

1994¢95

Action Packed WWF Ś Premium Cardboard

The embossed, raised-surface cards were unlike anything before. Bret Hart, HBK, Diesel, Razor Ramon RCs. On-card autographs signed by the talent. Under-the-radar set with incredible value hiding in it.

1995

Cardz WCW Main Event Ś The Competition Arrives

WCW gets its own card set as the Monday Night Wars heat up. Hulk Hogan has jumped to WCW. Sting. Ric Flair. And buried in this set Ś Steve Austin's first WCW card, one of the most undervalued RCs in the hobby.

1997

Cardz ECW Hardcore Heaven Ś The Holy Grail of Obscure Sets

ECW finally gets cards. Sandman. Tommy Dreamer. Taz. Sabu. And a young Rob Van Dam RC that almost nobody owned because ECW was underground. These cards are legitimately rare and the hobby is waking up to that.

1998¢99

Topps WCW/nWo Ś Peak Monday Night Wars

Goldberg at the top of his undefeated streak. The nWo at its peak. Rey Mysterio and Eddie Guerrero getting cards for the first time. These sets are the time capsule of wrestling's hottest period.

2000

Fleer WWF WrestleMania Ś The Attitude Era on Cardboard

Kurt Angle, Edge, Christian, The Hardy Boyz, Lita, Trish Stratus Ś all getting first major cards in one set. Plus The Rock, Stone Cold, Triple H. This is the most star-studded set of the era.

2001

The Line Ś Vintage Becomes Modern

Everything before 2001 is vintage. Everything after is modern. The hobby splits here. Vintage collectors chase wax and high-grade slabs. Modern collectors chase refractors, prizms, and autographs. Both are great hobbies.

2001¢2011

Topps WWE Takes Over

Topps becomes the exclusive WWE card licensee. Consistent annual releases build the modern checklist. The Ruthless Aggression era produces Cena RCs, Batista RCs, Orton early cards. Undervalued compared to what they'll be worth.

2012

The Modern Collector's Sweet Spot Begins

2012 Topps WWE is where modern collecting clicks. CM Punk on top. Daniel Bryan emerging. NXT becoming a thing. The sets from 2012¢2016 are some of the best value buys in modern wrestling cards right now.

2015

NXT Inserts Ś The Most Important Modern RCs

The 2015 Topps WWE NXT insert set includes the first Topps cards of Sasha Banks, Becky Lynch, Charlotte Flair, Bayley, Finn Bßlor, and Kevin Owens. These are the key cards of the current WWE landscape. Buy them now.

2021

Panini Prizm WWE Ś The Hobby Changes Forever

Prizm comes to wrestling and the hobby explodes. Silver prizms, color prizms, numbered parallels, on-card autos Ś the sports card boom hits wrestling hard. Prices surge. New collectors flood in. It's never been more exciting.

2021

Upper Deck AEW Ś The Alternative Gets Cards

AEW finally gets a card set. CM Punk and Bryan Danielson rookie cards in the same set. MJF. Darby Allin. Sting's AEW debut card. The set captures AEW at its absolute peak.

Today

The Best Time to Be a Wrestling Card Collector

More sets. More wrestlers. More parallels. More autos. And vintage cards are being rediscovered by a new generation. Whether you're chasing a 1985 Hogan or a 2024 Prizm Cody, there has never been a better time to be in this hobby.

Early WWF Years

From the 1982 Wrestling All-Stars sets through five iconic Topps releases — the era that put wrestling on cardboard nationally

WWF Topps Wrestling All-Stars Wax Packs

Before Topps, there were the Wrestling All-Stars sets. Produced in 1982 and 1983, these multi-promotion sets featured WWF, NWA, and AWA talent side by side — and for many legends like Andre the Giant, Bob Backlund, Jimmy Snuka, and Ric Flair, these are their true rookie cards. The 1985 Topps WWF set remains the most important nationally distributed wrestling card product, and it arrived at exactly the right moment: just weeks after the first WrestleMania, when Hulkamania was running wild across the entire country. Kids tore open wax packs at every candy counter in America. The hobby was born.

Topps followed with sets in 1986, 1987, 1988, and 1989, each capturing the Golden Era roster as it evolved. The Hart Foundation, The British Bulldogs, The Rockers, The Ultimate Warrior — the cards document the full roster of WWF's national expansion years. International editions from the UK, Australia, and other markets also exist for many of these sets, adding additional collectible variants.

Today, high-grade copies of key cards from the 1985 set routinely sell for hundreds — sometimes thousands — of dollars. The 1985 Topps WWF #1 Hulk Hogan in PSA 10 is one of the most coveted wrestling cards in the hobby.

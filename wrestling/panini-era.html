2022–2024 · Panini · WWE Exclusive License

Panini Era

Prizm comes to wrestling and changes everything — silver parallels, numbered rainbows, and the hobby's hottest three-year run

Panini Prizm WWE Select WWE Cody · Gunther · Rhea RCs

When Panini took the WWE card license in 2022, they brought their flagship Prizm brand with them — and the wrestling card hobby was never the same. Panini Prizm had already transformed basketball, football, and baseball collecting with its foil-based parallel rainbow system, and it did the same for wrestling. Suddenly collectors chasing Silver Prizms /299, Golds /10, and 1/1 Blacks were competing for the same Cody Rhodes and Gunther rookie cards that arrived in the first Panini WWE set.

The Panini era also produced Panini Select WWE, a tiered set with Concourse, Premier, and Field Level cards at different scarcity levels, plus on-card autographs and memorabilia cards. The three-year run (2022, 2023, 2024) documented the Roman Reigns Tribal Chief saga, Cody Rhodes finishing his story, CM Punk's return, Gunther's historic Intercontinental Championship reign, and Rhea Ripley's dominant Women's World Championship run.

Panini's WWE license ended after 2024 when Topps reclaimed the exclusive deal for 2025 onward. These three years of Panini WWE cards stand alone as a distinct and highly collectible era — the only time Panini ever had WWE, and the most investor-driven period in wrestling card history.

Panini Select WWE offered a tiered approach: Concourse (base tier, most common), Premier Level (mid-tier, slightly scarcer), and Field Level (top tier, most limited). Each tier has its own design within the same card. Select also featured on-card autos and Prizm-style parallels within each tier. It was produced for 2022, 2023, and 2024 alongside Prizm.

AEW Cards

All Elite Wrestling on cardboard — CM Punk RC, Bryan Danielson RC, MJF, Darby Allin, and the most undervalued modern RCs in the hobby

Upper Deck AEW Leaf

All Elite Wrestling launched in 2019 and became the first major competition to WWE in nearly two decades. When AEW finally got a trading card deal in 2021, Upper Deck delivered one of the most exciting sets of the modern era: a first-ever AEW card set that included CM Punk and Bryan Danielson rookie cards in the same product, alongside founding members like Kenny Omega, Chris Jericho, MJF, and The Young Bucks.

AEW cards represent one of the most undervalued corners of the wrestling card hobby. The CM Punk AEW RC from Upper Deck Series 1 is a legitimate key card — Punk hadn't been on wrestling television in nearly a decade before signing with AEW, and his first card in that return is historically significant. Bryan Danielson's AEW RC is equally important as the first card of his post-WWE career.

Leaf has also produced AEW-licensed products, and various independent manufacturers have released AEW-adjacent sets. This page covers all AEW card releases regardless of manufacturer.

Leaf Trading Cards has produced several AEW-related products, primarily focused on autographs and memorabilia cards rather than full base sets. Leaf AEW products typically feature on-card signatures from a smaller subset of talent and are often released as hobby-only box products. Key Leaf AEW releases include the 2022 Leaf AEW Autograph set and various multi-sport products that include AEW talent.

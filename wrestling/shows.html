Tips for Shopping at Card Shows

For New Collectors

  • Start with a wrestler you love, not a random set
  • Don't be afraid to ask questions — dealers love talking cards
  • The dollar box is your best friend at the start
  • Condition matters more than you think — check corners and edges
  • Check recent eBay sold listings before buying anything over $20
  • Bring a want list — shows move fast
  • Raw cards are often a better value for set building
  • Have fun. This hobby is supposed to be enjoyable.

For Experienced Collectors

  • Shows are the best place to find underpriced vintage
  • Talk to every wrestling card seller — we network
  • Bring your wantlist AND your trade binder
  • Early bird gets the worm — good stuff moves fast
  • Negotiate respectfully — dealers remember rude buyers
  • ECW cards are criminally undervalued — buy them now
  • 2012–2016 Topps WWE era is a sleeper — stock up
  • AEW cards are the most overlooked modern RCs in the hobby

Card Show Etiquette

The Unwritten Rules

  • Ask before opening binders or boxes — "can I look?" goes a long way
  • Handle cards carefully — pick them up by the edges
  • Don't lowball by 80% — it's insulting. Fair offers only.
  • If you're going to buy, commit. Don't hold cards for 20 minutes and walk away.
  • Support your local show — buy something even if it's a dollar card
  • Talk wrestling! The best part of the show is the conversation.
  • Bring cash — not every table takes cards
  • Bring kids — the hobby needs new collectors

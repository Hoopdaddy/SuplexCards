Notable Pre-1982 Sets

1955 Parkhurst Wrestling

48 cards. The gold standard of pre-1980 wrestling cards. Canadian distribution. Features Lou Thesz, Whipper Billy Watson, Gorgeous George, and the NWA stars of the era. Near-mint copies are virtually nonexistent.

NWA/CanadaExtremely Rare

1960s Wrestling Magazine Inserts

Various sizes and print runs. Distributed with "Wrestling Revue," "The Ring Wrestling," and regional wrestling magazines. Inconsistent quality but incredible historical record of the territory era.

Multi-PromoRare

Bruno Sammartino Promotional Cards

The longest-reigning WWWF Champion (nearly 8 years!) appeared on promotional photos and cards distributed at Madison Square Garden events. Not a true "set" — individual promotional pieces that function as early wrestling cards.

WWWFKey Figure

AWA Regional Cards (1970s)

Verne Gagne's American Wrestling Association produced promotional materials in the Upper Midwest. Cards featuring Gagne, Nick Bockwinkel, Ray Stevens, and a young Hulk Hogan (before his WWF fame) exist from this era.

AWA

UK & European Wrestling Cards

British wrestling had its own distinct stars and card releases during this era. Cards of Big Daddy, Giant Haystacks, Mick McManus, and other British wrestling legends were produced by various UK publishers throughout the 1960s and 70s.

UK/Europe

NWA Regional Promoter Cards

Jim Crockett Promotions, Georgia Championship Wrestling, Fritz Von Erich's WCCW — these regional NWA promotions all produced their own cards and photo sets for sale at arena events throughout the 1970s.

NWA Regional
1950s

Earliest Territory-Era Cards

Small-format black-and-white photo cards of NWA and regional wrestlers began appearing in the early 1950s. Gorgeous George — wrestling's first television superstar — appears on some of the earliest known cards. These were often promotional handouts at arenas or included with magazines.

1955

Parkhurst Wrestling (Canada)

Canadian card manufacturer Parkhurst — better known today for hockey cards — produced a set of wrestling cards featuring NWA and Canadian territory wrestlers. These 48-card sets are among the most collectible pre-1980 wrestling cards due to Parkhurst's established distribution and card quality. Key wrestlers: Lou Thesz, Whipper Billy Watson, Gorgeous George.

1960s

Magazine Insert Cards & Programs

Wrestling magazines like "Wrestling Revue" and "The Ring Wrestling" produced insert cards distributed with their publications. Regional promotions — Crockett Promotions, Fritz Von Erich's World Class Championship Wrestling, Verne Gagne's AWA — also produced their own promotional cards for sale at events.

1962

Wrestling All Stars (Various)

Multiple small publishers produced "Wrestling All Stars" style sets featuring the biggest NWA names of the era. Buddy Rogers (the original Nature Boy), Lou Thesz, and regional stars like Killer Kowalski and Freddie Blassie appear across various issues. Inconsistent print quality and tiny distribution make these exceptionally rare.

Late 1960s

WWWF Regional Issues

As the World Wide Wrestling Federation (the predecessor to WWF) began growing in the Northeast under Vince McMahon Sr., promotional materials including photo cards of WWWF stars began appearing. Bruno Sammartino — the longest-reigning WWWF Champion — appears on the earliest WWWF-branded cards. These are extraordinarily rare.

1970s

International & Food Issues

European and South American card manufacturers produced wrestling sets during the 1970s. UK-based manufacturers included wrestlers in "sports stars" multi-sport sets. In the US, food companies occasionally included wrestler cards with products. The 1970s also saw the rise of AWA-era Verne Gagne, Nick Bockwinkel, and a young Jesse Ventura on regional promotional cards.

1977–79

WWWF Transitional Era

As the WWWF transitioned to the WWF under Vince McMahon Jr., the first glimpses of future superstars appear on regional and international cards. Early Bob Backlund cards from this period predate the major Topps era by nearly a decade. The territory system was still intact, but its days were numbered.

A Note on Pre-1982 Collecting

Pre-1980 wrestling cards are not a traditional "set building" collecting category. There are no complete checklists — the cards themselves were produced inconsistently, often without numbering, by regional publishers whose records no longer exist. Collecting this era is more like archaeology than traditional card collecting. Online auction houses, estate sales, and wrestling memorabilia dealers are your best sources. Grading these cards for condition is especially important given how few survive.

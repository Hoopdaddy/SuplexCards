The Story of Wrestling Cards

Wrestling trading cards have been around almost as long as professional wrestling itself. From grainy black-and-white photos of territory-era wrestlers in the 1950s and 60s to the chrome refractors and Prizm parallels of today, the hobby has grown from a regional curiosity into a thriving collector's market.

The modern era truly began in 1985 when Topps released their 66-card WWF set � the first nationally distributed wrestling card product. Kids across America ripped wax packs hoping for a Hulk Hogan or Randy Savage. Those same cards in high grade now sell for hundreds of dollars.

The Monday Night Wars of the late 1990s created a card boom that mirrored the ratings war: Topps WCW/nWo Nitro sets, Cardz WCW, and the ultra-rare ECW Hardcore Heaven sets all exist because wrestling was the hottest thing in entertainment. Then came the Attitude Era's Fleer WWF sets, 17 years of Topps WWE dominance, and the Panini Prizm revolution that brought sports card investors flooding into wrestling.

This site documents all of it � every era, every set, every key rookie card. Use the checklists to track your collection across every era of the hobby.

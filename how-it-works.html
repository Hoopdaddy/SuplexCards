How It Works

Everything in the Registry is organized by IP — from the franchise landing page all the way down to individual films, series, and collectibles.

The Three-Level Structure

Level 1

Franchise Landing Page

Every major sci-fi and fantasy IP has its own dedicated landing page — the central hub for everything related to that universe.

Each franchise page includes:

  • ›A representative image and franchise overview
  • ›An in-universe timeline (or multiple timelines for complex IPs)
  • ›Two main sections: Media catalog and Collectibles catalog
  • ›Amazon affiliate links for both media and collectibles
See Example: Star Wars →

Level 2

Media List or Collectibles List

From the franchise page, you can drill into either the full media catalog or the full collectibles catalog.

Media L2

Lists all films, TV shows, comics, books, video games, and tabletop games in release-date order. Toggle to in-universe chronological order.

Collectibles L2

Lists all trading card sets, figure series, statue lines, prop replicas, and other collectibles. Each set links to a detailed L3 page.

Media Example → Collectibles Example →

Level 3

Individual Item Detail Page

The deepest level — a dedicated page for each individual film, episode, comic, game, figure, statue, or card set.

Media Detail

Summary, synopsis, cast and crew, release info, ratings, and Amazon links to purchase or stream.

Collectible Detail

Full checklist of figures, cards, and items in a set, with photos, rarity info, and Amazon purchase links.

Getting Started

1

Search or Browse a Franchise

Use the search bar on the home page to find any franchise by name — Star Wars, Dune, Lord of the Rings, and more. Or browse the full franchise grid to discover something new. Any keyword matching a franchise name will take you directly to that landing page.

2

Explore the Franchise Landing Page

The franchise page gives you a full overview of the IP — its timeline, all media types, and the collectibles that exist for it. Choose whether you want to dive into the films and TV shows, or explore the collectibles catalog.

3

Drill into Media or Collectibles

The media list shows every film, season, comic, book, and game in release order (with a toggle for in-universe chronology). The collectibles list shows every figure series, card set, statue, and prop replica.

4

View Individual Item Details

Click any item to see its full detail page — for films: synopsis, cast, and ratings; for collectibles: full checklists, rarity notes, and purchase links. Every detail page includes Amazon affiliate links to find and buy the item.

5

Coming Soon: Track Your Collection

Future versions of the Registry will let you mark items as owned or wanted, track your collection's value, and connect with other collectors in the community.
